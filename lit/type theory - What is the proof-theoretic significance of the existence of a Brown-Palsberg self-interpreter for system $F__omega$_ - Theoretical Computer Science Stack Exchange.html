    What is the proof-theoretic significance of the existence of a Brown-Palsberg self-interpreter for system FωFω?

    Ask Question
    Asked 5 years, 6 months ago
    Modified 5 years, 6 months ago
    Viewed 153 times

    This question does not show any research effort; it is unclear or not useful
    Bookmark this question.
    Show activity on this post.

    In A Self-Interpreter for F-omega, Brown and Palsberg construct for each term of each type x:Tx:T a representation x¯:□Tx¯:◻T (a metatheoretical function which can't be represented in the language), and an interpretation function uT:□T→TuT:◻T→T. This is reminiscent of GL provability logic, which in particular abstracts how PA can represent its own proof theory. But while the representation looks like the axiom ⊢T⟹⊢□T⊢T⟹⊢◻T, the second ⊢□T→T⊢◻T→T is inconsistent with GL.

    So what sort of gadget is this, in logical terms?

    Can it be used at all to talk about the metatheory of F-omega?

    What would a representation operation that satisfies the axioms of GL look like?

    There is no proof significance, since already Gödel's TT has a Brown-Palsberg self-interpreter for free. We need a better definition of a typed self-interpreter.
